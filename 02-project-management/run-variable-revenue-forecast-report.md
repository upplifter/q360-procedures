# Run the Variable Revenue Forecast Report

## What This Procedure Does

This procedure shows you how to run the Variable Revenue Forecast Report. The report projects variable revenue for future periods, helping you forecast income from projects and plan revenue recognition.

## Before You Begin

Ensure you have access to the Projects module and Reports section. You should understand your organization's revenue structure and which projects generate variable revenue. Have your forecast period ready before you start.

## Steps

### Step 1: Navigate to the Report

From the Main Menu, click **Projects**. In the Projects menu, click **Reports**. Select **Variable Revenue Forecast** from the report list.

### Step 2: Set Filter Criteria

The report filter panel opens with fields for defining your revenue forecast. Set the filters using the table below:

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|---------------|
| Project | Select specific projects or leave blank for all |
| Forecast Period | Choose the period you want to forecast (next quarter, next year, etc.) |
| Revenue Type | Select variable revenue, service revenue, or all revenue types |
| Division | Choose a division or leave blank for all |
| Status | Select Active, Pending, or All project statuses |

Click **Apply Filters** to update the report with your selections.

### Step 3: Run the Report

Click the **Run** or **Refresh** button to execute the report with your chosen filters.

### Step 4: Review the Report Output

The report displays forecasted variable revenue broken down by project and period. Review the totals and trends to understand projected income. Compare forecast amounts to your revenue targets and budget.

### Step 5: Export or Print (Optional)

To save the report, click **Export** and choose your format (Excel, PDF, or CSV). To print, click **Print** and follow your printer settings.

## What Happens Next

The forecast data displays current revenue projections based on your selected parameters. Use this information for revenue planning, forecasting, and financial analysis. Share the report with finance and sales teams for strategic planning.

## Common Issues

**Forecast revenue does not match expectations**
Review the forecast period and revenue types selected in your filters. Forecast accuracy depends on historical project data, so verify that comparable projects from the past are reflected correctly. Adjust filters and rerun the report to compare different scenarios.

**Some projects are missing from the forecast**
Confirm that the projects you expect to see are marked as Active and have revenue records. Projects with no historical revenue data may not appear in the forecast. Check your filter selections to ensure all desired projects are included.

**Forecast shows zero revenue for certain periods**
This may indicate that no revenue is projected for those periods based on current project schedules. Review project timelines and revenue recognition settings to verify the forecast is accurate. Contact your project manager if the forecast appears incorrect.

**Export file is incomplete or contains formatting errors**
Try exporting again with a different format (PDF instead of Excel, for example). If the issue persists, reduce the amount of data by filtering to fewer projects or shorter periods, then export the smaller dataset.

**Report filter selections do not persist after closing**
Report filters are temporary and reset when you navigate away. If you need to save specific filter settings for repeated use, note the filter values and reapply them each time you run the report.
