# Run the Project GP Backlog Projection Report

## What This Procedure Does

This procedure shows you how to run the Project GP Backlog Projection Report. The report forecasts your gross profit backlog trajectory, showing how profit backlog is expected to change over future periods based on current project activity and profit margins.

## Before You Begin

Ensure you have access to the Projects module and Reports section. You should understand your organization's gross profit structure and which projects you want to review. Have your projection period in mind before starting.

## Steps

### Step 1: Navigate to the Report

From the Main Menu, click **Projects**. In the Projects menu, click **Reports**. Select **Project GP Backlog Projection** from the report list.

### Step 2: Set Filter Criteria

The report filter panel opens with fields for defining your GP backlog projection. Set the filters using the table below:

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|---------------|
| Project | Select specific projects or leave blank for all |
| Projection Period | Choose the period you want to project (next quarter, next year, etc.) |
| Division | Choose a division or leave blank for all |
| Profit Center | Select a profit center or leave blank for all |
| Include Pending | Check to include pending or proposed projects in the projection |

Click **Apply Filters** to update the report with your selections.

### Step 3: Run the Report

Click the **Run** or **Refresh** button to execute the report with your chosen filters.

### Step 4: Review the Report Output

The report displays gross profit backlog projections over your selected time period. Review the trajectory to understand expected profit backlog changes. Examine individual project contributions to identify key profit drivers and margin trends.

### Step 5: Export or Print (Optional)

To save the report, click **Export** and choose your format (Excel, PDF, or CSV). To print, click **Print** and follow your printer settings.

## What Happens Next

The GP backlog projection data displays forecasted profit trajectory based on your selected parameters. Use this information for profitability planning, margin management, and financial forecasting. Share the report with finance and operations teams for strategic planning.

## Common Issues

**Projected GP backlog differs significantly from revenue backlog**
This is normal if your projects have varying profit margins. GP backlog reflects profitability while revenue backlog reflects sales value. Review project margins to understand the relationship between revenue and profit backlog.

**GP backlog projection shows declining profit over time**
This may indicate that scheduled projects have lower margins than historical projects or that cost escalation is outpacing revenue growth. Review project pricing and cost forecasts to verify the projection is accurate.

**Negative GP backlog values appear in the projection**
This can occur if projects have negative margins (loss-making contracts). Review these projects to confirm they are correct and understand the business drivers. Contact the project manager if the values appear incorrect.

**Certain projects are not included in the GP backlog**
Verify that projects are marked as Active or have pending status (if you checked the "Include Pending" option). Also confirm that the projects have gross profit information calculated. Check your filter selections to ensure all correct projects are included.

**Export does not include GP backlog calculations**
Ensure that the export format you selected supports all columns from the report. Try exporting to Excel instead of PDF if detailed data is not appearing. If issues persist, try a smaller data set with fewer projects.
