# Run the Profitability - Completed Projects Report

## What This Procedure Does

This procedure shows you how to run the Profitability - Completed Projects Report. The report analyzes the financial performance of closed and completed projects, helping you understand historical profitability and identify lessons learned.

## Before You Begin

Ensure you have access to the Projects module and Reports section. You should understand your organization's project completion and profitability metrics. Have your review period in mind before starting.

## Steps

### Step 1: Navigate to the Report

From the Main Menu, click **Projects**. In the Projects menu, click **Reports**. Select **Profitability - Completed Projects** from the report list.

### Step 2: Set Filter Criteria

The report filter panel opens with fields for defining your profitability analysis. Set the filters using the table below:

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|---------------|
| Project Status | Select Closed to view completed projects |
| Completion Date | Enter the date range for project completion |
| Division | Select a division or leave blank for all |
| Project Type | Select a project type or leave blank for all types |
| Profit Threshold | Enter minimum or maximum profit amounts to filter by |

Click **Apply Filters** to update the report with your selections.

### Step 3: Run the Report

Click the **Run** or **Refresh** button to execute the report with your chosen filters.

### Step 4: Review the Report Output

The report displays profitability data for completed projects, including revenue, costs, gross profit, and profit margin. Review the totals and averages to understand overall project portfolio performance. Examine individual projects to identify high-performing and underperforming projects.

### Step 5: Export or Print (Optional)

To save the report, click **Export** and choose your format (Excel, PDF, or CSV). To print, click **Print** and follow your printer settings.

## What Happens Next

The completed project profitability data displays final performance results based on your selected parameters. Use this information for post-mortem analysis, lessons learned, and strategic planning. Share the report with project management and finance teams for performance review and organizational learning.

## Common Issues

**Report shows no completed projects**
Verify that your completion date range includes closed projects. Check that you selected the correct project status filter. Adjust your date range and filters if needed and run the report again.

**Profit margins appear unusually high or low**
Review individual projects to confirm pricing, cost allocations, and revenue recognition are correct. Unusually high margins may indicate highly profitable projects or underestimated costs. Low margins may indicate competitive pricing or cost overruns. Investigate projects with extreme margins.

**Some completed projects are missing from the report**
Confirm that projects are marked as Closed in the system. Projects with other statuses will not appear in the completed projects report. Check with your project management team to verify project closure status.

**Revenue and cost amounts do not match project records**
Verify that all project transactions have been posted and that the report date range matches your review period. Incomplete transaction posting can cause discrepancies. Wait for period closing to complete before reviewing final profitability.

**Report takes a long time to generate**
If analyzing many completed projects, the report may run slowly. Narrow your selection by filtering to specific divisions, project types, or smaller date ranges. Run the report again with more focused criteria.
