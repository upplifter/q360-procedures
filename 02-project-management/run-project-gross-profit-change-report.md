# Run the Project Gross Profit Change Report

## What This Procedure Does

This procedure shows you how to run the Project Gross Profit Change Report. The report analyzes changes in gross profit between periods, helping you identify profit variances, margin changes, and profitability trends across projects.

## Before You Begin

Ensure you have access to the Projects module and Reports section. You should understand your organization's project profitability metrics. Have the periods you want to compare ready before you start.

## Steps

### Step 1: Navigate to the Report

From the Main Menu, click **Projects**. In the Projects menu, click **Reports**. Select **Project Gross Profit Change** from the report list.

### Step 2: Set Filter Criteria

The report filter panel opens with fields for defining your comparison. Set the filters using the table below:

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|---------------|
| Project | Select specific projects or leave blank for all |
| Current Period | Choose the latest period to analyze |
| Prior Period | Choose the previous period for comparison |
| Division | Select a division or leave blank for all |
| Status | Select Active, Closed, or All projects |

Click **Apply Filters** to update the report with your selections.

### Step 3: Run the Report

Click the **Run** or **Refresh** button to execute the report with your chosen filters.

### Step 4: Review the Report Output

The report displays gross profit amounts for both periods and calculates the change between them. Review the variance column to identify projects with significant profit improvements or declines. Examine the percentage change to understand the magnitude of variances.

### Step 5: Export or Print (Optional)

To save the report, click **Export** and choose your format (Excel, PDF, or CSV). To print, click **Print** and follow your printer settings.

## What Happens Next

The gross profit change data displays period-over-period comparisons based on your selected parameters. Use this information to analyze profitability trends, investigate margin changes, and identify projects that need attention. Share the report with project managers and finance teams for performance analysis.

## Common Issues

**Profit change calculations seem incorrect**
Verify that you selected the correct prior and current periods. Large changes may be due to project completion, significant cost adjustments, or revenue recognition timing. Review the individual projects to confirm the changes are accurate.

**Some projects show dramatic profit drops or improvements**
This can indicate project completion, major cost changes, or significant revenue adjustments during the period. Review the project details and transaction history to understand the changes. Contact the project manager if values appear incorrect.

**Report only shows a few projects**
Confirm that your filter includes the correct project status. Closed projects may not appear depending on your filter selections. Adjust your filters to include all project statuses you want to review.

**Prior period data is missing or shows zero**
Verify that your prior period selection includes dates when the projects were active and had financial activity. If projects are new, they may have no prior period data. Adjust your period selection to include periods with data.

**Variance percentages are very large or showing as infinity**
Large percentage variances can occur when comparing small prior period amounts to significantly different current period amounts. Review the absolute profit change in dollars to better understand the variance. Very large percentages usually indicate dramatic business changes, not errors.
