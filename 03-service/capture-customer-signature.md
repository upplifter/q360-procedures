# Capture Customer Signature on Call

## What This Procedure Does

Capture a customer's digital signature on a service call for documentation of work completion and authorization. The signature capture interface is optimized for mobile and tablet use. Once signed, the signature is stored on the call record and can be included on printed call reports.

## Before You Begin

- The service call must be open and accessible in the field
- A mobile device with touch screen (tablet or phone) is recommended for signature capture
- The customer must be present and ready to sign
- Work on the call should be complete or substantially complete before requesting signature

## Steps

### Step 1: Open the Service Call in the Field

Access the service call on your mobile device or field terminal. Navigate to the call details.

### Step 2: Access the Signature Capture Function

Click the Extended Menu (three vertical dots) on the action bar. Select Get Signature from the menu options. The signature capture window opens.

### Step 3: Present Device to Customer

Show the mobile device or tablet to the customer. The signature capture interface displays an open canvas ready for signature input.

### Step 4: Customer Signs on Screen

The customer uses a finger (on touch devices) or stylus to sign on the canvas. The interface is optimized for mobile devices and allows full-screen signature capture. The customer can see their signature in real-time as they write.

### Step 5: Accept or Reject the Signature

Review the signature with the customer. If acceptable, click Accept, Save, or OK (depending on your device interface). If the customer wishes to redo the signature, click Clear or Redo and have them sign again.

### Step 6: Confirm Signature Storage

The signature is saved to the call record automatically. A confirmation message appears. The signature is now linked to the service call and cannot be modified.

## What Happens Next

The signed call record is stored with the signature embedded. When you print a call report (see SVC-CALL-019), the signature can be included in the output by selecting the appropriate print options and template. The signature serves as proof of customer authorization and work completion. When the call is closed and invoiced, the signed documentation is available for records retention and customer disputes.

## Common Issues

**Signature capture window will not open on mobile device**

Ensure you are using the Extended Menu function and not a different method. If the window opens but does not display properly, try rotating your device from portrait to landscape. Refresh the page and try again. If the issue persists, contact your system administrator to verify mobile signature capture is enabled.

**Customer signature is illegible or incomplete**

Have the customer re-sign by clicking Clear or Redo in the signature capture window. Ensure the customer understands they are signing directly on the touch screen. If the customer has difficulty using touch input, try using a stylus if available. If signature capture continues to fail, document the completion in writing on the call (Problem Description or Solution Description fields) and note that signature was declined.

**Signature was saved but does not appear on printed report**

Verify the signature storage completed successfully (you should see a confirmation message). When printing the call report (see SVC-CALL-019), ensure you select a report template that includes signature and check the "Include Signature" option in the Print Report dialog. If the signature still does not appear, the call may not have saved properly; refresh the call and try re-capturing the signature.
